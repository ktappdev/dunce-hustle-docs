In Dunce Hustle, you aren't just competing on the leaderboard—you can actively hinder your opponents by mugging them.

## Mugging Mechanics

Attacking other players is high-risk, high-reward. You can target the same player multiple times per day, but diminishing returns push you to find new targets.

**Rules of engagement**
1. Cost: **10 Energy** per attempt (failed attempts cost 15 Energy).
2. Location: You must be at the **same Location** as your target.
3. Protection: **Net Worth < $2,000** cannot be mugged (Newbie Protection).

**Loot & diminishing returns**

Each successful mugging against the same player on the same day yields less loot. Failed attempts do **not** advance the penalty.

| Success # | Loot Penalty | Effective Loot |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1st | None | 100% (10–25% of cash/goods) |
| 2nd | -50% | 50% |
| 3rd+ | -75% | 25% (floor) |

**Outcomes**

- **Success:** Steal a portion of the target's **Cash** and **Inventory** per the table above; the target may also lose **1 Goon**.
- **Failure:** Lose **15 Energy** (instead of 10) and have a **50%** chance to lose a **Goon**.

**Dev notes / edge cases**

- **Zeroing out:** Players cannot be taken below **$2,000 net worth**.
- **Rounding:** If 25% of loot is less than 1 unit (e.g., $3 cash), it rounds down to **0**.
- **Notification:** Victims see a notice with the applied penalty (e.g., “You were MUGGED… (50% less)”).
